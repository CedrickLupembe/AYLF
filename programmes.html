OUR PROGRAMS

Currently, we are present in 12 university campuses with an average of 25 students per small group. We keep University students engaged with weekly conversations in the areas of Leadership, Friendship, and Jesus

We also seek to build a community of friends who care deeply about each other and are ready to walk together and hold each other accountable. The target is university students and student leaders. About 150 students participate in these weekly conversations in their different small groups on campus. By virtue of this, we commit to the following:

Weekly Campus Outreach

We are currently present in 12 University Campuses with an average of 25 students per small group. The main objective is to reach out to University students through weekly conversations on leadership, friendship and Jesus. We also seek to build a community of friends who care deeply about each other and are willing to walk with each other and hold each other accountable.

Small Group Coordinators Gathering

The gatherings are held to equip and build capacity of our small group coordinators so that they become more effective in coordinating the campus groups (Outreach programs). To remind the coordinators of the “why” we do AYLF so they can champion this in campuses. Target is to bring together two students (coordinator and assistant) from each of the universities we are present in and those who have expressed an interest in engaging.

City Forums

This brings together all those who have interacted with AYLF together for a one-day workshop/seminar on leadership, personal development, friendship and/or Jesus. This is important to keep the work grounded among those who have been part of it. Networking and reconnecting with friends also forms a great part of this forums. We target all AYLF members both in University and young professionals. We also open doors to anyone who would like to learn about our work.

Annual Gatherings

This brings together student leaders in Burundi, Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, Rwanda, DRC and South Sudan for a 4-day gathering. To have the participant attend the National Prayer Breakfast. To introduce the over 60% new participant to the AYLF philosophy. To engage them in conversations on leadership, friendship and Jesus. We also bring together Former Student Leaders and young professionals for the same retreat.

Community Outreach Program

We give LOVE and build HOPE to the hopeless and vulnerable communities and contribute to the development of the country through different public & community services in order to inspire, encourage and cultivate the spirit of giving back to the community in our young people

  • Public Clean ups and other public activities

    It builds patriotism spirit for young people and Inspires them to look for solutions of the problems in community within them and by themselves.

  • Humanitarian actions(Visits to the patients in the hospitals, shelter, health insurance/mutuel the sante, provision of food and other items of assistance for needy communities )

    to give love, build hope, save lives, alleviate suffering, put a smile on someone's face and maintain human dignity.
